Nil uses your device's microphone to detect the sound of your voice in real time. Every time you say a dhikr, the counter increments automatically — no tapping needed. All audio processing happens entirely on your device and is never stored or sent anywhere.

Yes, fully. Nil is entirely offline. All features — voice counting, tap counting, progress tracking, widgets, and Apple Watch — work without any internet connection. Your data lives only on your device.

The free version gives you unlimited dhikr types, voice and tap counting, and progress tracking. The premium version is a one-time purchase that unlocks Apple Watch support, home screen and lock screen widgets, and additional customisation options.

Long-press your iPhone's Lock Screen or Home Screen and tap the "+" button to enter edit mode. Search for "Nil" in the widget list and choose the size that suits you. The widget will show your current dhikr count and update in real time.

Once the Nil app is installed on your Apple Watch, it can track your dhikr sessions directly from your wrist. Counts sync automatically with the iPhone app via Apple's on-device App Group storage — no cloud, no account needed.

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you can delete it by removing the app. There are no accounts or servers to clear — once the app is deleted, all associated data is gone.
